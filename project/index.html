Welcome to Kanji Tools!



It is recommended that anyone who wants to become a fluent reader of the Japanese language should be familiar with the 2,136 general-use kanji. That being the case, kanji is an important part of studying the Japanese language. But for most kanji is difficult to learn. This site is intended for anyone who studies or has an interest in leaning Japanese kanji. It can be used as a kanji dictionary for look-ups by radicals, stroke count, English meaning, or Japanese onyomi or kunyomi readings. In addition, it can help the user to recognize and read Japanese kanji using generated quizzes and flash cards.
